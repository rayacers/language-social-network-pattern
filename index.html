Overview

Introduction

A common stereotype is that western people are more active on social media than eastern people, but how much truth there is in this sentence? Let's check. We propose to analyze the features’ distribution of different language users. Starting from common insights, that already available to everyone on the platform, like number of followers or total number of tweets we created new features, like daily mean number of tweetss and an internationality coefficient to say samething more with respect to the superficial differences. So we cluster the languages in two groups: Western and Eastern and we focused on their comparison. We take advangage of the study of paper [ADD A LINK] and In order to do so, we reproduce two propositions, 80-20 law, and cycardian law. Besides, we check the following preference of different language users. In the end, we apply machine learning algorithm both in a supervised and a unsupervised way to see if these differnce of behaviour are so marked that we can predict the culture of a user only based on the features of his/her own Twitter profile.

Datasets

EgoAlterProfiles: EgoAlterProfiles dataset contains profiles of two kind of users. One is ego which is uniformly sampled from Twitter API, and the other one is alter which is the follower or the followee of the ego. There are 34,006 egos and over 2,000,000 alters in this dataset. The profiles include followers counts, friends counts, statuses counts, and using languages of egos and alters.

EgoNetwork: EgoNetwork dataset contains the following relationship between egos and alters. There are over 4,000,000 following relationship in this dataset.

Note that the dataset only contains ego-alter following relationship but not ego-ego and alter-alter following relationship

EgoTimelines: EgoTimelines dataset contains information of tweets that generated by egos during a certain period. The information of tweets include create time, hashtag usage, mentioned ID etc.

Using behavior Analysis

In this section, we would like to know if eastern culture and western culture have different community using behavior on Twitter. In order to solve this problems, we then focus on three seperate questions.

Do both western and eastern culture follow 80-20 rules?

The 80-20 rule originally referred to the observation that 80% of Italy’s wealth belonged to only 20% of the population. From the replication we did before, we have already known that users on Twitter follow the 80-20 rule. In this analysis, we seperate users into western and eastern groups and see whether both two groups still follow 80-20 rule or a power law distribution in general.

Parameter N means that only the users who have posted more than N tweets are included. The selection of N does not influence the distribution qualitatively. The distribution of number of tweets per user is quite similar for both. And the law of 80-20% is still plausible.

Do western and eastern culture follow cycardian pattern on twitter?

The plot for the eastern and western cultures look quite different. But they still seem to follow the circadian rythm pattern as suggested in the paper.

But an interesting thing to note is that a peak in the number of tweets is visible around 22h for both west and east, but there is an equally high peak in the number of tweets around 13h in the east. Also, the number of active users gradually increase after 4h for both west and east. But there is a drastic dip in the number of active users between 15h and 21h in the east.

Moreover, western users have a lower activity on weekend, while eastern users do not have this characteristic.

Do users in different languages have specific following preference?

In this section, we would like to know if users in different languages have specific following preference. Therefore we sum up the following relationship between users in different languages, and we could know the following pattern of different language users. Here we only consider the following pairs which egos are the followers.

From the figures, we learn that people always like to follow accounts that use same language with themselves. It is obvious. However, we also find other interest facts:

- All users in different languages have a high tendency to follow English accounts.

- Western languages users have higher interest to English accounts that eastern languages users.

- People speak Portuguese and people speak Spanish have higher rate to follow each other comparing to other languages.

Besides, we introduce another feature, internationality. Internationality value is computed for each ego and it is equal to number of followers with an other language over the total number of followers.

From the figure, we could learn that people speak Gernamy have the largest internationality which means they have high interest to follow different languages accounts. On the other hand, people speak Japanese have the lowest internationality, which means they are less likely to follow different languages accounts.

Language Prediction

Conclusion

Do different language users have different characteristic on statistical results? ( i.e. activeness, popularity etc.)

From our results, we can clearly see that eastern culture have higher activeness and popularity on Twitter. Among all languages, people speak Arabic have most friends and most followers, while people speak Korean have least friends and followers. Besides, people speak Japanese have highest average daily tweets, while people speak Germany have lowest average daily tweets. To sum up, Arabic culture is most popular, while Korean culture is least popular. Also, Japanese is most active, while Germany is least active.

Do different culture (western and eastern) users have different using behaviors on Twitter?

80-20 law: Both eastern and western culture follows 80-20 law

Cycardian law: Both eastern and western culture follows cycardian law. However, eastern users are more active at lunch and and late night, while western users like to post tweet at around 8 p.m.

Following Preference: People always like to follow accounts that use same language with themselves. Moreover, All users in different languages have a high tendency to follow English accounts.
